Of all the harm to me that has come of the unconscionable act, the most impact was to my Faith; a lot of which is now lost to Fear!

Faith that in a nice mid-western city like Columbus, no one would consciously inflict harm on an unknown, unsuspecting, undeserving person. And in its place instilled the Fear in my heart, to not take safety and security for granted. And sure has imbibed the paranoia in my mind, to keep watching my back at all times; being overly concerned about sealing up my living spaces. Even having trouble sleeping and constantly checking doors and windows.

Now I'm back to being a stranger in a strange land, which dotes you with a false sense of security, while there are people who can just take it away from you.

To this day, I keep reliving that moment and a cold feeling of fear descends every time. I vividly remember that drizzling night. Despite being tired but having to finish up some work, I woke up after a short nap late at night, only to slowly realize that I had been robbed clean of all things of value in my house.

Noticing that my car keys were missing as well, I chose to go out to the parking lot, hoping against hope to see my car again, or at least if anything were strewn near where it stood. My car was not where I had parked it. But to my shock, in the corner of my eye, I see a car start up and back up onto the main road outside my community gate. My Car! Still Here!!

Vaguely comprehending what had just transpired, I was naively hoping to plead to the robber operating my vehicle to kindly return the few things that truly mattered to me; not my car, not even all my sellable stolen goods, but the past decade of my life, all in the data within my stolen devices and hard drives. Irreplaceable things that matter a lot to me: from personal documents, works of writing, all my photos & videos, artworks, to college papers and work projects: all the results of my hard-toiled labor, spending nights on end, all gone in a flash!

I was foolish to try and stop my own car, banking on the humanity of this unknown person in my driver's seat won't be cruel enough to drive over me, right? No, I was terribly wrong. Though everything happened within a few seconds, time runs painfully slow in my head. I paid for my error in judgment as the person chose, consciously chose to run over me! I tried to jump out of harm's way, but my foot still caught under the wheel, my shoulder on the mirror, and the accelerating car dragging me at its side, and the fortunate breaking of the mirror tossed me on the road, where I went tumbling and scrapping all over my hands and feet, lodging in me stones and splinters from the road. And he was driving off in front of my eyes, with a good chunk of my life's works and possessions.

On top of all my personal possessions that were robbed, the most valuable parts of my past decade, which means a whole lot more to me than any of the monetary losses as a result of this. Even if I could purchase the items again, how do you make up for all the lost personal memories?

For some of the items stolen which were not mine, though I knew it was not my fault, I felt so bad about it that I had to make reparations. All of these and even compiling these documentations have also cost me a considerable amount of time and effort sorting out everything. Is it fair to say that no one has a right to violate a home in this manner? The whole ordeal has left me shaken and distrustful of strangers loitering around my neighborhood and residences close to public roads.

The consequence of my injuries of being run over, dragged by my car and thrown on the side of the road were illness and incapacitation for a few weeks, followed by some medical procedures to remove debris lodged inside my hands and legs. The result of trauma in my feet limited my physical mobility for months after the incident. This cut down my activity and took a toll on my weight and skills. And this, in turn, has had a negative effect on my mental health and performance as well. Sometimes the people in my life have been the subject of my new paranoia and mood swings. But I am fortunate that I have understanding people in my life who want to help me through this.

All said, taking the other side, my wife and I have discussed and thought that if the thief had a family to feed, we did not want to trouble his home if it really comes from a place of need, but not greed. At the same time, I feel it is important for the law to take into account the effect this has had on victims like me and could have on others in the future if such perpetrators are encouraged by judicial leniency.

I bear no ill will towards anyone. If in the off chance that he has a change in heart and would return any of my stolen items that may still be in his orbit, especially parts of my digital life data, I would even plea to forgive him and I'll forget this whole affair as a bad accident.

The Lord works in mysterious ways, even if I don't know now, I sure hope there is something good that comes out of all this. I am glad to have not lost any limbs or worse still, my life, in this tribulation.

It is hard to see such things coming, even harder to think straight when faced with them. I will not forget that night of the incident anytime soon, but I wish I do.
