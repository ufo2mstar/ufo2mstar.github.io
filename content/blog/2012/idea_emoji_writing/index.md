Back in my Nokia 1100 days (best phone ever made, by the way - doubles as a brick at home!), texting had a natural expressiveness that got lost somewhere along the way. You'd fire off a message like:

> hey :) saw ur msg late :P was sleeping lol.. will call tomo ;) tc, ciao :bye

Every emoticon was a stage direction. The `:)` wasn't decoration - it was tone that is usually the hardest to communicate in text, Especially when the reader is not cognitively present enough to comprehend it. It told the reader *how* to hear the sentence. Without it, "saw ur msg late" reads apologetic or curt. With `:P` it reads playful, unbothered. The emoticon did the work that vocal inflection does in speech.

I had this idea I called **Emoji Writing** - what if we brought that same practice into longer-form text? Not the social media way, where emojis replace words or pile up at the end of a caption. More like how a screenplay has parentheticals *(sarcastically)*, *(beat)*, *(under his breath)* - except lighter, inline, and using the emoji vocabulary we already share.

## What It Looks Like

Plain text:

> I spent three hours debugging a typo in a config file. The fix was changing a single character. I closed my laptop and went for a walk.

With emoji writing:

> I spent three hours debugging a typo in a config file :expressionless: The fix was changing a single character :man_facepalming: I closed my laptop and went for a walk :relieved:

The emojis aren't adding information the words don't have. They're adding *tone* the words can't carry on their own. Was the walk angry or relieved? Was the debugging frustrating or funny in hindsight? The emoji disambiguates.
